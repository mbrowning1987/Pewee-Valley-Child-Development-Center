Playschool

The playschool room is for children 2.5 to 3.5 years old. These children are potty trained as well. In this classroom, children begin to really learn independence. Washing their hands, throwning away meals, dressing themselves and using more words to communicate with other children and teachers. They still do circle time and continue to learn their colors, shapes and letters. They also begin to learn how to reconize their names. Weather thats by names on chairs or cubbies or tracing their names. They begin to learn how to hold pencils, cut with scissors and use glue sticks. The art center is more open, and children can use the art supplies at will.
