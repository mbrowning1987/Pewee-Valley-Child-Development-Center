Transition's

We recognize that each infant has his or her own schedule. While we implement structure throughout the day, one of our primary goals is to keep your baby’s schedule on track. We encourage your instruction to keep your child’s routine. Each day includes breakfast, lunch and snack (for children on solid foods). They enjoy a morning circle time including singing and stories. Weather permitting we like to take walks in our buggy, we also like to get our tiny humans out for walks around the center. They also get time outside of the classroom in our gym. Where we have age appropriate toys, and jumpers. In this center, you will find an inviting and stimulating setting, with plenty of encouragement and praise. We aim to provide a variety of opportunities and activities that promote physical, social, emotional, cognitive, and creative development along side our Chirstian curriculum. Throughout their time in the transition room, the babies will be introduced to the following concepts daily. These concepts will help them transition into our toddler classrooms. By the time they move up into the toddler room, the child will show progress in or demonstrate the following: Develop fine motor skills and gross motor skills, recognition of popular children’s songs, introduction to socialization with other infants, discovering the world around them, communicating with each other, building of confidence and self-esteem, development of the individual personality, formation of a loving community, discovering my five senses, down to one nap a day on a cot, able to sit at a table and eat off a plate with utensils.
