The Toddler II's

Our toddler class is where socialization begins and the classroom setting is introduced. We provide a variety of activities to keep your toddler busy, happy, stimulated and occupied. We have center play, dramatic play, music, art, reading and more. It's very important for children to pretend play and learn to share and those are just some of the things we do in our toddler classrooms. Some of the main objectives of the toddler classrooms are working on gross motor skills such as jumping, rolling a ball, and fine motor skill, such as building blocks and coloring. Our older toddlers (18 months and up) will be introduced to potty training with a small toilet in the classroom, and will begin learning numbers, alphabet, shapes, colors, and how to socialize with other children. With extreme curiosity, our mission is to provide your child with enriched, age appropriate activities. With individualized attention and encouragement, this class is designed to prepare your child for our Preschool programs. Each day includes breakfast, lunch, and snack and also consists of structured activities such as: Circle time, crafts, story time, singing time, outdoor time (weather permitting) and gym time. In a positive environment, we strive to provide a variety of activities and experiences that promote physical, social, emotional, cognitive, and creative development all while instilling a chirstian curriculum.
